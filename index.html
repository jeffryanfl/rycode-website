The new rule

Everyone can build now.

Technical experience used to be the gate. It isn't anymore. AI agents turn creativity and need into working software — fast, cheap, and small. The people who win the next decade aren't the ones with the deepest engineering background. They're the ones who notice problems worth solving and have the nerve to ship a fix.

If you're still waiting for permission to build, you're already late.

About

Where AI meets vanilla code.

I'm Jeff — the builder behind Rycode. Rycode is my workshop for building real software out in the open, with AI as a creative collaborator rather than a crutch.

Every project here is designed, drafted, and debugged alongside AI — and then shipped by hand in vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No frameworks. No build tools. Just the fundamentals, moving faster than they used to.

The goal is simple: change the narrative. AI is not a shortcut around the craft. It's a partner that makes the craft more ambitious.

Operating principles

  • Build in the open. Each project ships live, even when rough.
  • AI is a collaborator. Not a crutch, not a gimmick — a creative partner.
  • Simple over clever. Readable code beats cute tricks.
  • Zero frameworks. Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, by choice.

How it's built

Four principles. Non-negotiable.

"Built openly with AI" sounds like a slogan. These four claims make it specific — and you can check the work.

01

AI writes first. I decide what ships.

Every line of code arrives as a suggestion. Nothing ships without a human read.

02

The stack is mine.

No frameworks, no build tools, no surprise dependencies. When AI suggests React, the answer is no — by design.

03

When AI is wrong, I push back — in writing.

The conversations behind this site include me telling AI to stop, rewrite, or simplify. The best version is usually the third draft, not the first.

04

Every bug gets debugged in the open.

Fixes land in the next commit — no polished demos hiding broken code.
